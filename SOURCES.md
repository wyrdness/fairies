# Sources & References

Comprehensive bibliography for fairy/Fae Folk phenomenon documentation.

---

## Books

### Academic & Folklore Studies

- **Briggs, K.** (1976). *An Encyclopedia of Fairies: Hobgoblins, Brownies, Bogies, and Other Supernatural Creatures*. Pantheon Books. ISBN: 978-0394409184
  - Comprehensive reference work covering fairy types, regional variations, and folklore traditions

- **Briggs, K.** (1967). *The Fairies in Tradition and Literature*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.
  - Academic study of fairy traditions across British Isles

- **Kirk, R.** (1691/1815). *The Secret Commonwealth of Elves, Fauns and Fairies*. Manuscript 1691; published 1815.
  - Earliest systematic account of Scottish fairy beliefs; primary historical source

- **Evans-Wentz, W.Y.** (1911). *The Fairy-Faith in Celtic Countries*. Oxford University Press.
  - Anthropological study of fairy belief in Ireland, Scotland, Wales, Brittany, Cornwall, and Isle of Man

- **Silver, C.** (1999). *Strange and Secret Peoples: Fairies and Victorian Consciousness*. Oxford University Press. ISBN: 978-0195102246
  - Examines Victorian-era transformation of fairy belief

- **Narváez, P.** (1991). *The Good People: New Fairylore Essays*. University Press of Kentucky. ISBN: 978-0813117478
  - Collection of scholarly essays on fairy folklore

- **Young, S.** (2018). *Fairy: The Coloring Book of Gods and Monsters Vol. 1*. CreateSpace.
  - Modern artistic interpretation with folklore notes

### Irish Fairy Traditions

- **Yeats, W.B.** (1888). *Fairy and Folk Tales of the Irish Peasantry*. Walter Scott.
  - Classic collection by Nobel Prize-winning poet; foundational Irish fairy folklore

- **Yeats, W.B.** (1892). *Irish Fairy Tales*. T. Fisher Unwin.
  - Additional collection of Irish fairy stories

- **Yeats, W.B.** (1893). *The Celtic Twilight*. Lawrence and Bullen.
  - Mystical accounts and folklore from western Ireland

- **Gregory, Lady A.** (1920). *Visions and Beliefs in the West of Ireland*. G.P. Putnam's Sons.
  - First-hand collection of fairy beliefs from Irish oral tradition

- **Ó Súilleabháin, S.** (1967). *Irish Folk Custom and Belief*. Cultural Relations Committee of Ireland.
  - Academic documentation of Irish folklore including fairy beliefs

- **Mac Manus, D.** (1959). *The Middle Kingdom: The Faerie World of Ireland*. Max Parrish.
  - Mid-20th century account of living Irish fairy belief

- **Lysaght, P.** (1986). *The Banshee: The Irish Supernatural Death Messenger*. Glendale Press.
  - Academic study of banshee tradition

### Scottish Fairy Traditions

- **Campbell, J.F.** (1860-1862). *Popular Tales of the West Highlands* (4 volumes). Edmonston and Douglas.
  - Classic collection of Scottish Gaelic folklore

- **Stewart, W.G.** (1823). *The Popular Superstitions and Festive Amusements of the Highlanders of Scotland*. Archibald Constable.
  - Early documentation of Highland fairy beliefs

- **MacGregor, A.A.** (1937). *The Peat-Fire Flame: Folk-Tales and Traditions of the Highlands & Islands*. Edinburgh: Moray Press.
  - Scottish oral tradition collection

- **Bruford, A. & MacDonald, D.A.** (1994). *Scottish Traditional Tales*. Birlinn. ISBN: 978-1874744696
  - Scholarly collection of Scottish folklore

### Welsh & Cornish Traditions

- **Rhys, J.** (1901). *Celtic Folklore: Welsh and Manx* (2 volumes). Oxford: Clarendon Press.
  - Academic study of Welsh and Manx fairy traditions

- **Sikes, W.** (1880). *British Goblins: Welsh Folk-lore, Fairy Mythology, Legends and Traditions*. Sampson Low, Marston, Searle & Rivington.
  - Comprehensive 19th century collection of Welsh fairy lore

- **Hunt, R.** (1865). *Popular Romances of the West of England; or, The Drolls, Traditions, and Superstitions of Old Cornwall*. John Camden Hotten.
  - Classic Cornish pixie and fairy traditions

- **Bottrell, W.** (1870-1880). *Traditions and Hearthside Stories of West Cornwall* (3 volumes).
  - Rich source of Cornish fairy folklore

### Breton Traditions

- **Sébillot, P.** (1904-1906). *Le Folk-lore de France* (4 volumes). Librairie orientale & américaine.
  - Includes extensive Breton fairy (korrigan) traditions

- **Luzel, F.-M.** (1868-1890). *Contes populaires de Basse-Bretagne* (3 volumes).
  - Breton folk tales including fairy stories

### Historical Cases & True Crime

- **Bourke, A.** (1999). *The Burning of Bridget Cleary*. Penguin Books. ISBN: 978-0140296136
  - Definitive account of 1895 Bridget Cleary changeling case in Ireland

- **Purkiss, D.** (2000). *Troublesome Things: A History of Fairies and Fairy Stories*. Allen Lane. ISBN: 978-0713993424
  - Historical analysis of fairy belief and its social impact

### Comparative & Cross-Cultural Studies

- **Vallée, J.** (1969). *Passport to Magonia: On UFOs, Folklore, and Parallel Worlds*. Contemporary Books. ISBN: 978-0809237920
  - Compares fairy abduction accounts to modern UFO encounters

- **Hartland, E.S.** (1891). *The Science of Fairy Tales: An Inquiry into Fairy Mythology*. Walter Scott.
  - Early comparative folklore study

### Literary & Cultural Studies

- **Briggs, K.** (1959). *The Anatomy of Puck: An Examination of Fairy Beliefs Among Shakespeare's Contemporaries and Successors*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.
  - Elizabethan fairy belief and literature

- **Latham, M.** (1930). *The Elizabethan Fairies: The Fairies of Folklore and the Fairies of Shakespeare*. Columbia University Press.
  - Renaissance fairy traditions

- **Ashliman, D.L.** (2004). *Folk and Fairy Tales: A Handbook*. Greenwood Press. ISBN: 978-0313328381
  - Academic reference guide

### Victorian Fairy Photography & Hoaxes

- **Cooper, J.** (1997). *The Case of the Cottingley Fairies*. Robert Hale. ISBN: 978-0709059134
  - Analysis of famous 1917 Cottingley fairy photographs

- **Smith, P.** (2007). *The Cottingley Fairies and the Men Who Fooled the World*. Tempus Publishing.
  - Historical context of Cottingley case

### Changeling Beliefs

- **Schoon, N.** (2017). *Fairy Tale: True Stories of the Cottingley Fairies, the Rhineland Mystic & a Mystery of the Ages*. The History Press.
  - Includes changeling belief history

- **Eberly, S.S.** (1988). "Fairies and the Folklore of Disability: Changelings, Hybrids and the Solitary Fairy." *Folklore*, 99(1), 58-77.
  - Academic analysis of changeling beliefs and disability

## Historical & Primary Sources

### Medieval Manuscripts

- *Lebor Gabála Érenn* (The Book of Invasions) — 11th century Irish manuscript
  - Records mythology of Tuatha Dé Danann

- *The Táin* (Táin Bó Cúailnge) — 8th century Irish epic
  - Features supernatural beings and Sidhe

- *The Mabinogion* — Welsh medieval tales
  - Includes fairy encounters and otherworld journeys

- *Sir Orfeo* — Middle English Breton lai (c. 1300)
  - Fairy king abduction story

### Early Modern Sources

- **Kirk, Rev. R.** (1691). *The Secret Commonwealth of Elves, Fauns and Fairies*. [Manuscript]
  - Most important primary source on Scottish fairy beliefs

- **Burton, R.** (1621). *The Anatomy of Melancholy*.
  - Contains sections on fairy belief

- **Reginald Scot** (1584). *The Discoverie of Witchcraft*.
  - Skeptical account of fairy beliefs

### Witch Trial Records

- Scottish witch trial records (16th-17th centuries)
  - Many accused claimed fairy contact; documented in:
  - *Survey of Scottish Witchcraft Database* (www.shca.ed.ac.uk/witches/)

- **Pitcairn, R.** (1833). *Criminal Trials in Scotland* (3 volumes).
  - Includes fairy-related testimonies

### 19th Century Collections

- **Croker, T.C.** (1825-1828). *Fairy Legends and Traditions of the South of Ireland* (3 volumes).
  - Early Irish fairy tale collection; influenced Brothers Grimm

- **Keightley, T.** (1828). *The Fairy Mythology: Illustrative of the Romance and Superstition of Various Countries*. William Harrison Ainsworth.
  - Comparative fairy lore encyclopedia

## Databases & Archives

## Recommended Reading Order

### For Beginners
1. Briggs, K. — *An Encyclopedia of Fairies* (reference)
2. Yeats, W.B. — *Irish Fairy Tales* (stories)
3. Bourke, A. — *The Burning of Bridget Cleary* (historical case)

### For Academic Study
1. Kirk, R. — *The Secret Commonwealth* (primary source)
2. Evans-Wentz, W.Y. — *The Fairy-Faith in Celtic Countries* (anthropology)
3. Briggs, K. — *The Fairies in Tradition and Literature* (scholarly)
4. Silver, C. — *Strange and Secret Peoples* (Victorian context)

### For Cultural Context
1. Gregory, Lady A. — *Visions and Beliefs in the West of Ireland*
2. Mac Manus, D. — *The Middle Kingdom*
3. Regional collections (Campbell, Rhys, Hunt, etc.)
